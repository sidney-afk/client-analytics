> Owner directive, recorded at the owner's request ("the most important part is what
> I'm telling you right now"). This document preserves the intent behind the
> enterprise-atlas work so any future session can recover not just *what* was asked,
> but *why*. Distilled faithfully from the owner's own words; nothing here is
> agent-invented strategy.

## The vision, in the owner's terms

- **A good project is everything well done in small quantities.** The details matter
  (a content calendar that works is a small detail made of smaller components that
  each have to work) — and we invest heavily there. What we have *not* done is take
  the step back: the bigger picture, which "always includes also the smaller picture
  and the details."
- **The step back has many angles, and both matter:**
  - *The company angle*: we are a marketing company looking for clients. What is the
    company? What is it doing? How does it work? Which are its parts? What type of
    clients are we looking for?
  - *The software angle*: is what we built the best we can do? Do we do things
    correctly, or is there a better way?
- **First milestone = understanding + documentation, not improvement.** "I would be
  happy with only at least documenting what we are in all of those senses. Improving
  it would be better after." Document how things work so there is a base to build on.
- **The vault / hive mind.** Like the website map, but for everything possible: "a
  huge single source of truth that can be improved every time," always adapting when
  the website or the company changes. Its purpose is shared understanding between
  the owner and every AI session — so sessions stop paying the audit tax and get
  quick access to how things actually work.
- **Slice big ideas into sessions.** When a project is too big for one session,
  split it across sessions whose results flow back into the vault. Sessions are
  workers; the documented single source of truth is the memory.

## The angle space (owner-named + amplified)

The owner named two angles as *examples* and directed (2026-07-18, second brief) that
examples be treated as pointers into a space, not as the list. The amplified space,
filtered for value — each is a floor of the atlas:

|---|---|---|---|

| 2 | Software craft | owner | Is what we built the best way to build it? (Documented first; judged later — P4.) |
| 3 | Client's chair | amplified | The journey as the client lives it, ad → results — every touchpoint, end to end. |
| 4 | People | amplified | Who does what; where knowledge concentrates in one head; what only the owner / only Kasper can do. |

| 7 | Data | amplified | What data the company owns, where it lives, what is sensitive, what is backed up, what would hurt to lose. |

Excluded on purpose: market/competitive analysis (pure owner knowledge → interview
questions, not a floor); growth/scalability (folds into 5+6).

## Standing doctrine this creates

1. **Atlas-first**: big-picture understanding lives in a maintained atlas whose
   floors link down into the existing maps (ecosystem/funnel map, client lifecycle
   map, the SyncView truth layer). Do not duplicate a floor that exists — link it.
2. **Sessions feed the vault**: any session that learns something durable ships it
   into the documented layer before it ends. Ephemeral understanding is waste.
3. **Documentation is for retrieval, not ceremony**: structure everything so a
   cold-start session finds what it needs in minutes ("quick access to the
   information" is the stated goal — optimize for that, not for length).
4. **Examples are pointers** (owner directive, 2026-07-18 second brief): when the
   owner illustrates a concept with examples, the examples locate the space — they
   are not the list. The agent's job is to enumerate the space the examples point
   into, filter for value, and present the amplified map (marking what the owner
   named vs what was amplified, so provenance stays honest). Locking onto the
   literal example is a failure mode. This method is operationalized as step one
   of `.claude/skills/skill-forge` and applies to ALL owner briefs, not only skills.
